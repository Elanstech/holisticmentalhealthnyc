Individual Therapy

Personalized one-on-one support for your mental health journey

What to Expect

Individual therapy provides a confidential, safe space to explore your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. Sessions are tailored to your unique needs and goals, focusing on personal growth and healing.

  • Session Length: 50 minutes
  • Frequency: Weekly or bi-weekly
  • Approach: Personalized treatment plan
  • Methods: Evidence-based therapeutic techniques
  • Progress: Regular assessments and adjustments

Before Treatment

Preparing for therapy helps maximize the benefits of your sessions:

  • Free Consultation: 15-minute phone call to discuss your needs and answer questions
  • Intake Assessment: Comprehensive evaluation during your first session to understand your background and goals
  • Goal Setting: Collaboratively identify specific areas you'd like to address in therapy
  • Insurance Verification: We'll help verify your insurance coverage and discuss payment options
  • Paperwork: Complete intake forms and consent documents before your first session

During Treatment

What happens in your therapy sessions:

  • Safe Environment: Non-judgmental space to share openly and authentically
  • Practical Tools: Learn coping strategies and skills you can use daily
  • Emotional Processing: Work through difficult emotions and past experiences
  • Pattern Recognition: Develop insights into recurring thoughts and behaviors
  • Skill Building: Build resilience, emotional regulation, and self-awareness
  • Homework Assignments: Practice new skills between sessions

After Treatment

Long-term benefits and continued growth:

  • Improved Wellbeing: Greater emotional stability and life satisfaction
  • Coping Skills: Effective tools to manage stress and challenges
  • Self-Awareness: Deeper understanding of yourself and your patterns
  • Better Relationships: Enhanced communication and connection with others
  • Maintenance Plan: Strategies to maintain progress and prevent relapse
  • Follow-Up Support: Optional check-in sessions as needed

Couples Counseling

Strengthen your relationship and deepen your connection

What to Expect

Couples counseling helps partners improve communication, resolve conflicts, and build a stronger, healthier relationship. We create a neutral, supportive environment where both partners feel heard and valued.

  • Session Length: 60 minutes
  • Frequency: Weekly sessions recommended
  • Approach: Evidence-based couples therapy
  • Methods: Gottman Method, EFT, Communication Training
  • Format: Both partners present for all sessions

Before Treatment

Preparing for couples counseling:

  • Joint Consultation: Both partners meet with therapist to discuss concerns and goals
  • Relationship Assessment: Comprehensive evaluation of relationship dynamics and patterns
  • Commitment Agreement: Both partners commit to the therapeutic process
  • Individual Sessions: Optional brief individual meetings to discuss private concerns
  • Goal Alignment: Define shared objectives and desired outcomes
  • Ground Rules: Establish guidelines for respectful communication

During Treatment

What happens in couples therapy:

  • Communication Skills: Learn effective listening and expression techniques
  • Conflict Resolution: Address disagreements in constructive, healthy ways
  • Trust Building: Rebuild or strengthen trust and emotional safety
  • Intimacy Enhancement: Deepen emotional and physical connection
  • Pattern Recognition: Identify and change negative interaction cycles
  • Homework Exercises: Practice new skills and strengthen bond between sessions

After Treatment

Long-term relationship benefits:

  • Improved Communication: Better understanding and expression of needs and feelings
  • Stronger Bond: Deeper emotional intimacy and connection
  • Conflict Management: Effective tools to navigate disagreements
  • Renewed Commitment: Strengthened partnership and shared vision
  • Maintenance Strategies: Ongoing practices to keep relationship healthy
  • Check-In Sessions: Optional periodic sessions to maintain progress

Family Therapy

Create harmony and strengthen family bonds

What to Expect

Family therapy addresses dynamics and relationships within the family system. We work together to improve communication, resolve conflicts, and create a healthier, more supportive family environment.

  • Session Length: 60-90 minutes
  • Frequency: Weekly or bi-weekly
  • Approach: Family systems therapy
  • Methods: Structural, strategic, and narrative therapy
  • Participants: All family members or specific combinations

Before Treatment

Preparing your family for therapy:

  • Family Consultation: Initial meeting with all participating family members
  • Family Assessment: Understanding family dynamics, roles, and patterns
  • Individual Check-Ins: Brief one-on-one sessions with each member if needed
  • Age-Appropriate Approach: Tailored communication for children and teens
  • Treatment Goals: Collaborative goal-setting involving all members
  • Participation Agreement: Commitment from all family members

During Treatment

What happens in family therapy sessions:

  • Family Communication: Improve how family members talk and listen to each other
  • Conflict Resolution: Address family tensions in healthy, productive ways
  • Role Clarification: Understand and adjust family roles and boundaries
  • Parenting Support: Strengthen parenting skills and co-parenting strategies
  • Sibling Dynamics: Address and improve sibling relationships
  • Life Transitions: Navigate major family changes together

After Treatment

Long-term family benefits:

  • Enhanced Communication: Open, honest dialogue among all family members
  • Healthier Dynamics: More balanced, supportive family relationships
  • Better Problem-Solving: Effective skills to navigate future challenges
  • Stronger Bonds: Deeper emotional connections within the family
  • Family Rituals: Established practices to maintain family wellness
  • Ongoing Support: Resources and strategies for continued growth

Mindfulness-Based Therapy

Find peace through present-moment awareness

What to Expect

Mindfulness-based therapy integrates meditation and awareness practices with traditional therapy to reduce stress, manage anxiety, improve emotional regulation, and enhance overall wellbeing.

  • Session Length: 50-60 minutes
  • Frequency: Weekly sessions
  • Approach: MBSR, MBCT, DBT mindfulness
  • Methods: Meditation, breathing exercises, body scans
  • Practice: Daily homework and mindfulness exercises

Before Treatment

Preparing for mindfulness therapy:

  • Initial Assessment: Discuss your stress levels, anxiety, and mental health goals
  • Mindfulness Introduction: Learn the basics of mindfulness and its benefits
  • Expectation Setting: Understand that mindfulness is a skill developed over time
  • Comfortable Space: Identify a quiet place at home for daily practice
  • Open Mind: Approach with curiosity and without judgment
  • Commitment to Practice: Willingness to practice daily between sessions

During Treatment

What happens in mindfulness therapy:

  • Guided Meditation: Learn various meditation techniques in sessions
  • Breath Work: Practice breathing exercises for stress relief
  • Body Awareness: Develop connection to physical sensations
  • Present-Moment Focus: Train your mind to stay in the now
  • Thought Observation: Learn to observe thoughts without judgment
  • Daily Integration: Apply mindfulness to everyday activities

After Treatment

Long-term mindfulness benefits:

  • Stress Reduction: Significantly lower stress and anxiety levels
  • Emotional Balance: Greater emotional regulation and stability
  • Improved Focus: Enhanced concentration and mental clarity
  • Better Sleep: Improved sleep quality and relaxation
  • Self-Awareness: Deeper understanding of thoughts and emotions
  • Lifelong Practice: Tools you can use forever

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

Evidence-based treatment for lasting change

What to Expect

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a structured, goal-oriented approach that helps you identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors. It's one of the most researched and effective forms of therapy.

  • Session Length: 50 minutes
  • Frequency: Weekly sessions
  • Duration: Typically 12-20 sessions
  • Approach: Structured, goal-focused treatment
  • Methods: Cognitive restructuring, behavioral experiments

Before Treatment

Preparing for CBT:

  • Initial Evaluation: Comprehensive assessment of current challenges and symptoms
  • Goal Definition: Clearly define specific, measurable treatment goals
  • Baseline Measures: Track mood, thoughts, and behaviors for comparison
  • Psychoeducation: Learn about the CBT model and how thoughts affect feelings
  • Commitment to Homework: CBT requires practicing skills between sessions
  • Active Participation: CBT is collaborative and requires engagement

During Treatment

What happens in CBT sessions:

  • Thought Identification: Recognize automatic negative thoughts
  • Cognitive Restructuring: Challenge and change distorted thinking patterns
  • Behavioral Activation: Increase positive activities and reduce avoidance
  • Exposure Therapy: Gradually face fears in a safe, controlled way
  • Skill Practice: Learn and practice coping strategies
  • Progress Tracking: Monitor symptoms and celebrate improvements

After Treatment

Long-term CBT benefits:

  • Reduced Symptoms: Significant decrease in anxiety, depression, or other symptoms
  • Cognitive Skills: Ability to identify and challenge negative thoughts
  • Behavioral Tools: Effective strategies to change unhelpful behaviors
  • Relapse Prevention: Skills to prevent symptom return
  • Self-Therapy: Ability to be your own therapist
  • Lasting Change: Research shows CBT effects are long-lasting

Group Therapy

Healing through shared experiences and community support

What to Expect

Group therapy brings together people with similar challenges in a supportive, confidential environment. Led by a trained therapist, groups provide unique benefits through shared experiences and peer support.

  • Session Length: 90 minutes
  • Frequency: Weekly sessions
  • Group Size: 6-10 participants
  • Format: Structured topics and open discussion
  • Duration: Ongoing or time-limited (8-12 weeks)

Before Treatment

Preparing for group therapy:

  • Individual Screening: One-on-one meeting to assess fit for group
  • Group Selection: Match you with appropriate group based on needs
  • Confidentiality Agreement: All members commit to confidentiality
  • Group Guidelines: Learn expectations and group norms
  • Anxiety Normalization: It's normal to feel nervous at first
  • Optional Individual Therapy: Can combine with one-on-one sessions

During Treatment

What happens in group therapy:

  • Peer Support: Connect with others who understand your struggles
  • Shared Experiences: Learn you're not alone in your challenges
  • Multiple Perspectives: Gain insights from diverse viewpoints
  • Skill Practice: Practice new behaviors in a safe environment
  • Feedback: Receive and give constructive support
  • Social Skills: Improve interpersonal communication

After Treatment

Long-term group therapy benefits:

  • Reduced Isolation: Feel more connected and less alone
  • Social Confidence: Improved comfort in social situations
  • Support Network: Lasting connections with group members
  • New Perspectives: Broader understanding of your challenges
  • Accountability: Ongoing motivation from peer support
  • Cost-Effective: More affordable than individual therapy
